Acceptable Use Policy

Effective Date: January 10, 2026
Last Updated: January 10, 2026

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") outlines the rules and expectations for using Tipwave Technologies, LLC's ("Tipwave," "we," "us," or "our") platform, hardware devices, and related services.

By using Tipwave, you agree to comply with this AUP. Violation of this policy may result in suspension or termination of your account, removal from the platform, and potential legal action.

1. Purpose of This Policy

Tipwave is a hardware and software platform designed for live performers to receive tips and song requests from their audiences through an elegant, real-time experience. We want to create a safe, respectful, and lawful environment for all users.

This policy ensures that Tipwave is used in a way that:

  • Respects the rights and safety of performers, audiences, and the community
  • Complies with all applicable laws and regulations
  • Maintains the integrity and security of our hardware devices and platform
  • Protects the experience and emotional connection that Tipwave creates

2. Prohibited Uses

You may not use Tipwave for any of the following purposes:

2.1 Illegal Activity

  • Engaging in or promoting illegal activity, including fraud, theft, money laundering, or any other criminal conduct
  • Violating any local, state, national, or international law or regulation
  • Using Tipwave to facilitate the sale of illegal goods or services

2.2 Fraud and Deception

  • Providing false or misleading information when creating an account
  • Impersonating another person or entity
  • Using stolen credit cards, payment methods, or personal information
  • Creating fake accounts or using bots to inflate tips or engagement

2.3 Harassment and Abuse

  • Harassing, threatening, stalking, or intimidating other users
  • Sending offensive, abusive, or hateful messages or requests
  • Engaging in any form of discrimination based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or any other protected characteristic

2.4 Harmful Content

  • Promoting or glorifying violence, terrorism, or self-harm
  • Sharing content that is sexually explicit, obscene, or exploitative
  • Distributing malware, viruses, or other harmful software
  • Engaging in hate speech or incitement to violence

2.5 Misuse of Hardware

  • Do not tamper with the device. Do not disassemble, modify, repair, or attempt to reverse-engineer your Tipwave hardware device.
  • Do not alter functionality. Do not attempt to change how the device responds to tips, disable security features, or manipulate the firmware.
  • Illegal hardware use. Do not use the device for illegal purposes or to facilitate illegal activity.
  • No unauthorized transfers. Do not sell, gift, loan, or transfer your device to another person without Tipwave's written consent.
  • Venue compliance. You are responsible for ensuring your device use complies with venue policies, local regulations, and any applicable permits or licenses.

2.6 Payment System Abuse

  • Attempting to circumvent platform fees or payment processing systems
  • Initiating chargebacks or disputes in bad faith
  • Using stolen or fraudulent payment methods
  • Manipulating tips or transactions to defraud performers or audiences

2.7 Spam and Unwanted Communications

  • Sending unsolicited commercial messages or advertisements
  • Spamming performers or audiences with excessive or irrelevant content
  • Using Tipwave to collect user data for marketing purposes without consent

2.8 Interference with Service

  • Attempting to hack, disrupt, or interfere with the operation of Tipwave
  • Overloading our servers or networks with excessive requests
  • Using automated tools or scripts to access or manipulate the platform without permission

3. Respectful and Lawful Use

3.1 Be Respectful

Tipwave is a community built on mutual respect and appreciation. We expect all users to:

  • Treat performers and audiences with kindness and courtesy
  • Respect others' privacy and personal boundaries
  • Engage in constructive and positive interactions

3.2 Comply with Laws

You are responsible for ensuring that your use of Tipwave complies with all applicable laws, including:

  • Tax laws (performers must report and pay taxes on their earnings)
  • Employment and labor laws (if you employ others in your performances)
  • Content and licensing laws (if you perform copyrighted material)
  • Data protection and privacy laws

3.3 Performer Responsibilities

As a performer, you are solely responsible for:

  • The content of your performances
  • Any interactions with your audience
  • Ensuring that your performances comply with local laws and venue regulations

Tipwave is not responsible for the content of performances or any disputes that arise between performers and audiences.

4. Reporting Violations

If you believe someone is violating this Acceptable Use Policy, please report it to us immediately:

Email: support@tipwave.live

Please include:

  • A description of the violation
  • The username or account information of the person involved
  • Any relevant screenshots, messages, or evidence

We will investigate all reports and take appropriate action, which may include:

  • Issuing a warning
  • Suspending or terminating the offending account
  • Reporting illegal activity to law enforcement

5. Consequences of Violations

Violating this Acceptable Use Policy may result in:

5.1 Warning

For minor or first-time violations, we may issue a warning and ask you to correct your behavior.

5.2 Suspension

For more serious or repeated violations, we may temporarily suspend your account while we investigate.

5.3 Termination

For severe violations, we may permanently terminate your account and ban you from using Tipwave.

5.4 Legal Action

If your conduct violates the law or causes harm to Tipwave or other users, we may pursue legal remedies, including reporting you to law enforcement.

6. Intellectual Property

You may not use Tipwave to infringe on the intellectual property rights of others. This includes:

  • Uploading or sharing copyrighted content without permission
  • Using trademarks, logos, or branding that belong to someone else
  • Plagiarizing or stealing creative work

If you believe your intellectual property rights have been violated on Tipwave, please contact us at support@tipwave.live.

7. No Guarantee of Service

While we strive to provide a reliable and secure platform, we do not guarantee that Tipwave will be available at all times or free from errors. We reserve the right to:

  • Modify or discontinue features or services at any time
  • Suspend or terminate accounts for violations of this AUP
  • Update this policy as needed to address new risks or challenges

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Acceptable Use Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will notify you by email or through the Tipwave platform. Your continued use of Tipwave after the changes take effect constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.

The "Last Updated" date at the top of this page indicates when this policy was last revised.
